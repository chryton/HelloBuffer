Oh, Hello Buffer!

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

I've been using Buffer pretty religiously for almost this many:

And I quickly grew to love not only the app but also the awesome people behind the app:

So, after reading your blogs, tweets, and interviews, I have come to one conclusion:

I want to work with you.

Why would you want to work with me? Let me give you a few reasons ↝

I helped save babies in Belize (true story).

Philosophy & History B.A. + Interactive Media M.A. = Insatiable urge to learn and tackle problems large and small.

Your emphasis on transparency and willingness to try new things is at the core of what I believe in and is something that is desperately needed in our industry (both product-wise and agency-wise).

Your position as both thought leaders and feature leaders is diffcult to maintain but rewarding both from a personal and a consumer standpoint as you are not afraid to continue learning and are able to improve people and your product at every level.

Your responsiveness to customers is indicative of a company who focuses on not only the big picture of the product but also that there are real users with real, legitimate problems and you may not always have it right the first time; this speaks volumes to your company's character.

While in graduate school I ran a team of 4 to help deliver a site and social media package for the Citizens Foundation in Iceland within a two-week timeframe. This reinforced the fact that at heart I am a generalist and feel comfortable being a project manager, development project manager, developer, UX designer, and content manager among anything else that is needed.

Learning and trying new things are a huge part of who I am and I can see from your blog posts and the job descriptions, learning and growth is a huge part of what Buffer is about and that is a culture I want to contribute to.

I have Tron-like tendencies. (I fight for the user!)

So why go through this elaborate setting just to get a job?

Well, I don't want a job. I want something that I am excited to wake up to and work on. I want something that respects my time as much as I respect it's time. I want to work as a team rather than a department.

I want to work with you. Not for you.

If all this gets is a few interesting conversations, then even though I will be disappointed that I didn't get to join the team, I will have at the very least had a dialogue with a group of people that I deeply respect and will give me a bit of perspective.

So I guess you have a couple of options now:

1. Ignore this approach to starting a conversation with you.

2. Contact me, tell me I am crazy person.

3. Contact me, tell me I am crazy, then see #1.

4. Contact me, find out more about how I will bring world domination to Buffer, and send me to Buffer Bootcamp (I have a duffel packed, though I may need to take out my climbing equipment).

Thank you for taking the time to look at this site.
